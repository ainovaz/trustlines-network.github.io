Why People Powered Money?

People Powered Money basically means complementary currencies, which are currencies supplementing the legal tender issued and controlled by formal central entities such as commercial or central banks.

People Powered Money has benefits for individuals, local communities, businesses and the economy as a whole. Please see greater implications to see an elaboration of some of these benefits.

We believe People Powered Money fits particularly well in our context because trustlines money in fact is issued in a decentralised way and informally collateralised by people's interpersonal relations of trust. This means people indeed have a choice whether they want to support a given currency or not.

What does IOU mean?

An IOU (I owe you) is a promise to pay whatever account measure has been agreed upon, back at a later point in time. In the Trustlines Network, we refer to IOU's within a specific currency network as Trustlines Money. These can be denominated in a real world currency, a commodity, a crypto token or any other unit of account users may agree upon.

What is a creditline?

A creditline is set up between two trusting entities (e.g. friends), allowing one party to be indebted to the other.

When setting up a creditline in the trustlines network, the users sign an agreement on the Ethereum blockchain, which specifies 'in what measure and how much' the creditor allows the debitor to be indebted with, of which the debtor also signs he is obligated to pay off the debt?

How does a trustline work?

When both parties have provided each other with a credit line in the same type of trustlines money (e.g. Euros) it is a bilateral agreement and is then called a trustline. The beauty of having several existing trustlines within a currency network is the ability to facilitate cheap, secure and fast transactions routing through a chain of trustlines existing between friends-of-friends-of-friends.

In essence this enables everyone to issue currencies to make global payments through ones interpersonal trusted social network.

Why several currency networks?

The possibility to participate in several currency networks makes a user capable of benefiting from both local resilient and global interconnected networks. One user can for example decide to set up a currency network for a local 'circular economy' network that has inbuilt demurrage, a mutual-credit element, and does not allow for interests.

The trustlines network supports a variety of ways of customising currency networks, of which there are still many to be explored. The platform moreover supports ERC20 tokens.Trustline money in a default currency network can be exchanged for ETH and vice versa on an on-chain exchange. Therefore one can always tell what the value of trustline money is compared to other currencies.

Can I create money out of nothing?

The short answer is yes.

Similar to a fractional reserve commercial bank, you are able to create money, symbolising future promises out of nothing. This does however far from guarantee somebody will accept these promises. The money you create is only as much worth as the value your trustlines connections have decided to trust you with.

When individuals issue credit they are, contrary to sales employees in banks, personally liable. Not only will friends also be more understanding if you miss a payment, they probably also know your spending habits a lot better than banks. We believe this incentivizes a higher degree of financial responsibility.

How can Trustlines help the unbanked?

While the unbanked are increasingly getting smartphones, they still cannot get financial services because both mobile applications and traditional banks require government ID/cash deposits or credit cards to work. The Trustlines Network enables the unbanked access to payments without the needing any of that this is made possible by credit issuance through the social graph of every user.

The Trustlines Network provides the unbanked the means for payment, exchange, savings, loaning as well as access to future financial empowering tools. Interpersonal relations often governs credit issuance in many unbanked societies and the Trustlines Network is based on the same simple and proven mechanisms found in for example, the Hawala system.

The big difference to these current informal offerings is the automatic accounting system, mutually agreed upon conditions, as well as the tamper-proof documentation of who owes what to whom.

Why is Trustlines useful for local circular economies?

Local currencies, specific to a community, offer a way to keep money circulating increasing the economic activity within the realm of the community. This is contrary to pumping 'international homogeneous' currencies into an area, which tends to flow straight back to the source, without making any sustainable economic community impact.

At the same time, communities tend to have a demand for goods or services provided by outside suppliers, which means they also need to be able to pay in currencies that they trust and accept.

This dilemma requires that communities use more than one currency which is exactly what the Trustlines Network enables. This means a community can both conduct circular trade within a customised local and resilient currency while also being able to participate in interconnected currency networks.

How can businesses benefit from trustlines?

Setting up a business currency network can provide businesses with interest-free liquidity to enhance the mutually beneficial exchange of goods and services. Businesses can therefore reduce the reliance on legal tender and expensive banking services and only spend legal tender for operational costs outside of the business network.

In periods of economic recession such business networks can indeed also provide a counter-cyclical stabilisation mechanism, insofar that they guarantee a minimum of economic activity in an event of low fiat liquidity in circulation.

How is the concept different from a LETSystem?

The Trustlines Network can be viewed as an interconnected global network of LETSystems in which each subsystem is managed by a single individual.

A classic LETSystem is centralized and only tracks obligations to and from central intermediaries in a mutual-credit fashion. In effect, this means if someone defaults, the loss of credit is shared with everyone, thus arguably only making it suitable for small communities.

With Trustlines, obligations are dedicated to specific individuals who agreed to trust you, holding you personally accountable. We believe personal trust relations based on individual reliability provides a stronger incentive for individuals to honor their obligations than in a mutual-credit based system.

In addition comes the benefit of automatic accounting, tamper-proof documentation as well as the option to enable currency network interoperability.
